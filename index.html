Frequently Asked Questions

Enigma AI is a modular, open-domain AI assistant focused on transparency, ethical design, and user privacy. Unlike many commercial AIs, it is designed for local deployment, academic research, and full user control.
Enigma AI does not retain user data unless explicitly permitted. All logs are user-controlled and can be deleted at any time. Privacy is a core design principle.
You can run Enigma AI locally on your own hardware, ensuring full control and privacy. Cloud deployment is optional for those who need scalability.
Enigma AI is designed to integrate with open-source LLMs such as Mistral-7B, GPT-J, and fine-tuned LLaMA, with a plug-and-play architecture for easy model swapping.
You can contribute via GitHub or contact the project lead for academic or industry collaborations. Open-source contributions and feedback are welcome!
Ethical considerations include privacy, bias mitigation, explainability, and compliance with regulations like GDPR. Enigma AI is built with these in mind.
The system includes bias testing, explainable output logs, and user feedback mechanisms to ensure responsible and transparent AI behavior.
Yes, Enigma AI is open source and encourages community contributions and transparency in development.
Enigma AI is intended for researchers, students, developers, and organizations interested in ethical, user-centric AI systems.
You can open an issue on GitHub or contact the project lead via email for support or to report bugs and suggestions.
